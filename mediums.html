Different types of traditional Drawing Styles

Graphite Pencils

Used since the 17th century. Graphite pencils can be used for sketching, outlining, or shading. Graphite pencils vary in hardness that can be used in fine arts. Allows for easy erasing and remaking of work.

Colored Pencils

Colored pencils have come from childhood to being a professional medium in art. They gained attention because of the numerous advantages they offer. Colored pencil artwork is expressive, deep, and luminescent. It also allows for photo-realistic pieces.

Charcoal

Charocal is one of the oldest mediums in drawing. It is made of powedered organic material bond with wax. Charcoal is used to make expressive lines. This material is easily erasable and smugable.

Chalk

Similar to charcoal, chalk usualy comes in three versions, black, white, and saguine, depeding on material. Black chalks have soft black stone, white is made from limestone and sanquines have red ochre. Chalks became their own medium during the Renaissance.

Pastels

Medium consist of paint pigments simialr to oil paints and a binding material. There are different types of pastels; soft pastels are great for blending, hard pastels for sharp outlines or large compositions. Pan pastels are a modern version that is applied with a soft sponge and water-soluble pastels for thinner layers of semi-transparent colors that belnd easily.

Pen and ink

An art medium where pen is used for outlining and adding color in art forms like pen art, lettering, and caligraphy. There are graphic pens, fountain pens, or drafting pens
